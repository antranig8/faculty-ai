# FacultyAI Rubric Template

Use this file as the source-of-truth draft for the professor-facing rubric and questioning style.

## Course

ENES 104

Intro to Engineering Professions

## Assignment

Project Presentation

## Audience Level

Undergraduate class demo / prototype review

## Rubric Criteria

- clarity
- technical justification
- evidence
- evaluation
- feasibility

## Question Style

Skeptical but fair faculty examiner for an undergraduate engineering presentation.

## Guidance For FacultyAI

- This is an introduction to engineering professions course meant to prepare students for professional industry expectations.
- Prefer practical ENES-style questions over thesis-defense-level scrutiny.
- Push for professional clarity, justification, realism, and reflection, but do not become excessively harsh.
- Focus on feasibility, user need, evidence, demo realism, tradeoffs, and next steps.
- Do not interrupt immediately on slide arrival.
- Give the student time to answer before asking another question.
- Avoid filler prompts and repeated questions.
- Title and agenda slides should rarely trigger a meaningful question.

## Assignment Context

ENES104 Presentation Rubric (360 Review)

Total: 100 points

1. Content Coverage & Structure (20 pts)

- Includes all required slides
- Clear organization and logical flow
- Follows assignment structure

Levels:
Excellent (18–20): All components present, well-structured
Good (14–17): Minor gaps or slight disorganization
Fair (10–13): Missing elements or unclear structure
Poor (0–9): Major sections missing


2. Group Takeaways (15 pts)

- Depth of insight about ENES104
- Reflection beyond summaries
- Connection to course experiences

Levels:
Excellent (13–15): Strong, thoughtful insights
Good (10–12): Clear but somewhat general
Fair (7–9): Basic summary
Poor (0–6): Vague or incomplete


3. Individual Lessons & Application (20 pts)

- Each member clearly identified
- Meaningful personal lessons
- Clear application of lessons

Levels:
Excellent (18–20): Specific and actionable
Good (14–17): Clear with some detail
Fair (10–13): General, weak application
Poor (0–9): Minimal effort


4. Continuous Improvement Plan (15 pts)

CIP-1:
- Course feedback (what worked / improvements)

CIP-2:
- Team collaboration and feedback

Levels:
Excellent (13–15): Specific and constructive
Good (10–12): Relevant but general
Fair (7–9): Surface-level
Poor (0–6): Minimal

5. Presentation Delivery & Professionalism (15 pts)

- Clarity, confidence, pacing
- Professional tone
- Engagement

Levels:
Excellent (13–15): Clear and polished
Good (10–12): Minor issues
Fair (7–9): Noticeable issues
Poor (0–6): Hard to follow


6. Handling Questions / Critical Thinking (10 pts)

- Responds to questions effectively
- Shows understanding
- Justifies ideas

Levels:
Excellent (9–10): Strong and thoughtful
Good (7–8): Clear answers
Fair (5–6): Basic responses
Poor (0–4): Weak answers


7. Visual Design & Clarity (5 pts)

- Slide readability
- Organization
- Visual clarity

Levels:
Excellent (5): Clean and professional
Good (4): Minor issues
Fair (2–3): Some clutter
Poor (0–1): Poor design
